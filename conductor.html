Guest Conductor

Dr. Jill Burgett

Professor and Director of Choral Activities at the University of Northern Colorado
"A distinguished conductor and scholar whose artistic leadership has graced prestigious stages across Europe, North America, and Asia."

Dr. Jill Burgett serves as Professor and Director of Choral Activities at the University of Northern Colorado (UNC). A leader in choral excellence, she conducts the UNC Concert Choir and Chamber Choir while overseeing the graduate-level choral methods and conducting curricula. Prior to her tenure at UNC, she held the position of Associate Director of Choral Activities at Ball State University.

An accomplished academic, Dr. Burgett earned her Doctor of Arts from Ball State University, where she was honored as the recipient of the Don Neuen fellowship. She further holds a Master of Music from BSU and a Bachelor of Music from Heidelberg University. Her professional journey is rooted in two decades of conducting experience within the Indiana public school system, fostering a deep commitment to music education.

Dr. Burgett’s leadership extends into the broader choral community, having served on the boards of Indiana and Colorado ACDA (as President), the Central Division, and the National Collegiate Choral Festival. As a prolific clinician and speaker, she is a frequent guest conductor for All-State Choirs and her own ensembles have been featured at prominent ACDA conferences across the United States.

Her international artistic presence is truly global. She has led the UNC Chamber Choir on tours through the cultural hearts of Barcelona, Paris, Vienna, Prague, Budapest, and Rome. Beyond conducting, she is a sought-after international lecturer, sharing her expertise in Beijing, Rome, Mexico City, Puebla, London, and Bangkok. Her research specialized in Latin American and Catalan choral music, as well as refined rehearsal and conducting techniques, continues to influence choral pedagogy worldwide.
